Leaving Hong Kong

It’s never too early to start planning for your pet move. Typically your pet will require a microchip, valid rabies & annual inoculations and a health check prior departure.

For certain destinations, it can however, be a very difficult and long process, places such as Australia, New Zealand, Japan and Taiwan can take up to 8 months to get everything arranged and they have very strict rules on documentation and timings of blood tests that are required.

With our in depth knowledge, we are here to take away the worry and stress, so the sooner you call us, the sooner we can get everything arranged for you to make your pet move stress and trouble free.

Moving to Hong Kong

If you are planning on moving to Hong Kong we can help you with a door to door service or destination services upon arrival.

The import requirements vary slightly and have been spilt into the following 3 groups.

Group 1 Countries

Dogs and cats imported direct from U.K., Ireland, Australia, New Zealand, Fiji, Hawaii and Japan are normally exempted from quarantine. Dogs and cats must be over 60 days old, be identified with a microchip, be fully vaccinated against feline/canine infectious diseases and be issued a health certificate within 14 days prior departure.

Group 2 Countries

Dogs and cats imported from Austria, Bahamas, Bahrain, Belgium, Bermuda, Brunei, Canada, Cayman Island, Cyprus, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Gibraltar, Guam, Iceland, Italy, Jamaica, Luxembourg, Maldives, Malta, Mauritius, Norway, New Caledonia, Papua New Guinea, Portugal, Seychelles, Singapore, Solomon Islands, South Africa, Spain, Sweden Switzerland , The Netherlands, USA

Group 3 Countries

All other countries are subject to a minimum of four months quarantine at the Animal Management Center.
Dogs and cats must be over 60 days old, be identified with a microchip, be fully vaccinated against feline/canine infectious diseases and be issued a health certificate within 14 days prior departure.
